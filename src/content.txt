Trading Education Syllabus
Program Objective:
To provide a structured, progressive trading education that builds strong financial foundations, develops technical analysis skills, and introduces indicator-based trading while preventing common beginner and amateur mistakes.
###################################

###################################
Goal: Build core financial literacy and prevent beginner mistakes
Instructor Lead: Palani

-Definition and purpose of trading

-Cryptocurrencies

#########################################
Goal: Teach learners how to read and interpret price action
Instructor Lead: Palani

Learning Outcomes

-Identify market trends and ranges

Topics Covered

-OHLC (Open, High, Low, Close) structure

-Downtrends

Module 3: Indicator-Based Trading (Amateur Level)#
##################################################
Goal: Teach proper indicator usage and avoid common amateur errors
Instructor Lead: Dylan

Learning Outcomes
-By the end of this module, learners will be able to:
-Understand the intended purpose of indicators
-Apply indicators correctly within market context
-Avoid over-reliance on single indicators
-Identify low-quality indicator signals

Topics Covered
1. Indicator Purpose vs Reality
-What indicators are designed to show
-Common misconceptions

2. Relative Strength Index (RSI)
-Momentum interpretation
-Mean reversion concepts
-Overbought and oversold limitations

3. MACD
-Trend confirmation
-Momentum shifts
-Signal line interpretation

4. Bollinger Bands
-Volatility measurement
-Expansion and contraction
-False breakout risks

5. Indicator Confluence
-Combining indicators responsibly
-Why one indicator is never sufficient

6. Common Indicator Mistakes
-Indicator stacking
-Late trade entries
-Curve fitting and over-optimization

7.Interactive Elements
-Indicator toggle functionality on live charts
-Signal labeling exercises
-Indicator-based trading challenges
